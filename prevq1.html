05 — Key Insights

A quarter of
remarkable growth

Eight highlights from Q1 2026 that define where Apars Classroom stands — and where it is headed next.

Milestone
FRB-26 Became a Movement

The HSC 26 Final Revision Batch crossed 15.8 million views in a single quarter — with 108K students actively engaged and an exceptional 12.4% bounce rate. It is one of the most-watched educational programmes in Bangladesh this year.

Organic Growth
Half a Million Students Found Us Naturally

Over 80% of new students arrived through direct visits or organic search — a testament to word-of-mouth trust and the platform's growing reputation. Apars Classroom is becoming the first name students type when they need to prepare.

Engagement
Students Stay for Nearly 15 Minutes Per Visit

An average engagement time of 14 minutes 34 seconds per active student reflects genuine, focused learning — not passive browsing. Students are watching, revising, and returning. That depth of engagement is rare at this scale.

Next Wave
HSC 27 Is Already Surging

Physics, Higher Math, and Chemistry courses for the HSC 27 batch collectively drew hundreds of thousands of views this quarter — before the peak season has even begun. The next cohort of students is already here, and growing fast.

Retention
Students Keep Coming Back

WAU/MAU stickiness reached 50.5% by end of quarter — meaning one in two monthly students is back every single week. DAU/MAU climbed from under 6% in January to nearly 20% by March, showing the platform builds habit the longer a batch runs.

Reach
Students from Every Corner of Bangladesh

Dhaka leads with 165K students, but Chattogram, Rajshahi, Khulna, Cumilla, Sylhet, Mymensingh, Bogura, and Rangpur collectively represent a massive and growing share. Apars Classroom is not a capital-city platform — it belongs to the entire country.

Scale
1.89 Billion Requests, Delivered Reliably

Across three months, the platform's delivery infrastructure handled 1.89 billion requests and served over 1.13 petabytes of content — with origin response times improving by 26% between January and March. The infrastructure scaled with demand.

Mobile-First
Built for the Student in Their Pocket

72.8% of all students accessed the platform on Android — entirely through the web browser, no app required. Apars Classroom delivers a full learning experience to any student with a smartphone, regardless of device tier or internet plan. In Q2 we are launching our Android Application.
