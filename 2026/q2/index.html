06 — Key Insights

A quarter of growth beneath the surface

Eight takeaways from Q2 2026 — what the numbers say about where Apar's Classroom stands, and where it is heading.

Milestone

A new audience high — 299K monthly students

Rolling 30-day active students closed June at 299K, the highest point of the year, climbing steadily through the back half of the quarter. The audience is growing even as individual exam-season viewing eased off.

Signal

More students, lighter load — by design

Delivered bandwidth fell from 429 TB to 273 TB across the quarter while students grew. That's not fewer people — it's a shift from heavy exam-revision streaming to lighter, browse-first study as the HSC 26 cycle wound down.

Product

Mid-June: short-form arrives

From June 14, engagement per student stepped up and origin traffic doubled overnight — the fingerprint of fresh short-form video entering the catalogue. New content, higher engagement, and a new caching challenge, all at once.

Launch

The Android app is live

Q2 delivered on the Q1 promise: the native Android application shipped, already registering millions of in-app events alongside the web platform. Students now reach ASG Shop on the surface that fits them.

Content

FRB-26 stayed the beating heart

The HSC 26 Final Revision Batch drew 16.8M views with a remarkable 14.3% bounce rate — one of the most-watched educational programmes in Bangladesh, and the anchor of the quarter's engagement.

Loyalty

Three in four are returning students

Returning students held roughly 75% of daily activity, with weekly stickiness peaking at 55% of monthly actives. This is a habit-formed base, not a churning one — students come back on their own.

Reach

A platform for the whole country

Dhaka leads with 196K students, but Chattogram, Rajshahi, Khulna, Sylhet, Rangpur, Bogura and beyond make up a growing majority. Apar's Classroom belongs to all of Bangladesh, not just the capital.

Scale

1.82 billion requests, delivered clean

Over three months the edge network served 1.06 petabytes across 1.82 billion requests at a 92.7% cache hit rate, with origin response improving to 212 ms by June. The infrastructure scaled quietly with demand.
